👋 Hi, I'm Michele, a quantitative researcher with 7+ years across quantitative psychology, data science and applied ML. I also use NLP to pull explainable signal out of open-ended text, so qualitative data feeds straight into analysis.

## What I do

🧪 **Experimentation and causal inference.** Designs that separate real effects from noise: A/B-style testing, mixed-effects models (lm, lmer, glmer), quasi-experimental designs, sampling and randomization, dimensionality reduction (clustering, PCA, LDA, t-SNE, UMAP).

📊 **Survey science and psychometrics.** Building and validating measurement instruments at scale (sometimes tens of thousands of respondents) and checking they behave the same across countries and demographics, so group comparisons hold up.

🧩 **Segmentation and dimensionality reduction.** Clustering and PCA to find meaningful segments and turn high-dimensional data into patterns people can act on.

🤖 **Applied ML and NLP.** Mostly Python (pandas, scikit-learn, statsmodels) and R. Text classification pipelines and language-model evaluation, with the model's reasoning kept visible.

🧠 **Machine psychology.** Testing LLMs like ChatGPT or Claude with the experimental methods psychologists use on people, to see how they behave under controlled conditions and how far their outputs can be trusted.

🕸️ **Complex network science.** Mapping language and behavior as networks of connected concepts, which surfaces structure that standard statistics tend to miss.

🎧 **Human factors engineering.** I've measured brain activity directly with EEG and used non-invasive brain stimulation to study how attention and performance shift under real task load. Same question UX and human factors ask, answered with physiological data: what drives performance, and where does it break down?

🗣️ **Mixed methods.** Semi-structured interviews and moderated sessions alongside the numbers, so the qualitative picture and the data inform each other.

## Some examples

📈 Modeled individual-level behavioral variability with multivariate stats, mixed-effects models (lmer/glmer), clustering and PCA across 180+ participant sessions. One analysis found a repeatable 10% performance gain and traced it to a single cause, giving the team a clear call on which method to keep.

✅ Validated a 42-item behavioral instrument on 39,775 respondents. Classical psychometrics plus ML feature selection (Random Forest) let us shorten the survey without losing predictive power, which matters once respondent fatigue is a concern.

🌍 Established measurement invariance across sex and country on a multilingual sample of 1,420, so a difference between two groups reflects real behavior rather than how the survey was read.

🧠 Built an NLP pipeline that extracts psychological, numerical and linguistic features from text, linking survey-measured behavior to signal in sources like user reviews: which emotions show up and how intensely, and whether a behavioral trajectory can be predicted.
